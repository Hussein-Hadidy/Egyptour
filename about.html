Geography

Egypt is located in northeastern Africa and southwestern Asia, bordered by:

  • Libya to the west
  • Sudan to the south
  • The Red Sea to the east
  • The Mediterranean Sea to the north
  • Gaza to the northeast

Most of Egypt is desert. The main fertile area is along the banks of the Nile River, the longest river in the world. Egypt also includes the Sinai Peninsula, which bridges Africa and Asia.

Climate

  1. Summer (June – August): Very hot, temperatures up to 40°C in the south.
  2. Autumn (September – November): Mild and pleasant, great for travel.
  3. Winter (December – February): Cool in the north, mild in south. Best tourist season.
  4. Spring (March – May): Warm, with occasional Khamsin sandstorms.

The best time to visit Egypt is October to April when temperatures are comfortable for alot of activities.

Overview

Egypt is one of the world's oldest civilizations, dating back over 5,000 years. Known as the "Gift of the Nile", ancient Egyptians built one of the greatest empires in human history. They were pioneers in writing (hieroglyphics), mathematics, medicine, and architecture. Their monuments — the Pyramids, the Sphinx, and hundreds of temples — still stand today.

Modern Egypt

Today, Egypt is a modern Arab republic with a rich blend of ancient tradition and modern life. Cairo, the capital, is one of the largest cities in Africa and the Middle East. Egypt is a member of the United Nations and the African Union. Tourism is one of Egypt's most important industries, attracting millions of visitors from around the world every year.

Why Visit Egypt?

  • See the only remaining Wonder of the Ancient World — the Great Pyramid of Giza
  • Cruise the Nile River between Luxor and Aswan
  • Dive in the crystal-clear waters of the Red Sea
  • Explore the Valley of the Kings where pharaohs were buried
  • Walk through the ancient city of Alexandria founded by Alexander the Great
  • Experience the vibrant markets of Khan el-Khalili in Cairo
  • Taste delicious Egyptian food including Koshary, Ful Medames, and more
  • Watch the sun set over the desert from the top of a sand dune
