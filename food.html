Featured Dishes

A closer look at Egypt's most iconic foods

Koshary — Egypt's National Dish

Main Course  |  Street Food

Koshary is considered the national dish of Egypt — a hearty, filling, and completely vegetarian dish made by layering several different ingredients. It is one of the most popular and affordable street foods, found on almost every street corner in Cairo.

Ingredients:

  • White rice
  • Brown lentils
  • Macaroni (short pasta)
  • Spiced tomato sauce
  • Crispy fried onions
  • Chickpeas & garlic vinegar

Egyptian Breakfast — The Breakfast of Champions

Breakfast  |  5,000 Years Old

Egyptian breakfast is one of the richest in the world, built around Ful Medames (fava beans) — a staple eaten in Egypt for over 5,000 years. The spread typically includes Ful, Taameya (falafel), eggs, cheese, and fresh bread, enjoyed by millions every morning.

Common Items:

  • Ful Medames (cooked fava beans)
  • Taameya (Egyptian falafel)
  • Fresh eggs & white cheese
  • Aish Baladi (Egyptian flatbread)
  • Olive oil, lemon & tomatoes

Hawawshi — Egypt's Favorite Sandwich

Snack  |  Street Food

Hawawshi is one of Egypt's most beloved street foods — a crispy baked bread stuffed generously with spiced minced meat. It is found in every corner shop and restaurant, and is a staple at family gatherings and street stalls alike.

Ingredients:

  • Minced beef or lamb
  • Onion, parsley & green pepper
  • Egyptian spice blend
  • Baked inside Egyptian baladi bread
  • Served hot and crispy

Egyptian Seafood — Mediterranean & Red Sea Catch

Main Course  |  Alexandria Specialty

Egypt's long Mediterranean and Red Sea coastlines make seafood a major part of the national cuisine. Alexandria in particular is famous for its fresh grilled fish, calamari, and shrimp. Seafood restaurants line the Corniche waterfront serving the daily catch.

Popular Options:

  • Grilled fish (sea bass, mullet, bream)
  • Calamari & shrimp
  • Fish tagine with vegetables
  • Served with rice, salad & tahini
