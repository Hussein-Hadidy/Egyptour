Featured Hotels

Detailed information on Egypt's most celebrated hotels

Cairo Hotels

Marriott Mena House — Cairo

★★★★★ $300–$600 / night

Built in 1869 as a royal hunting lodge, the Marriott Mena House is one of the world's most iconic hotels. It offers direct views of the Great Pyramids of Giza from its rooms and pool. Historic guests have included US presidents, kings, and celebrities.

Features: Pyramid-view rooms, outdoor pool, multiple restaurants, spa, tennis courts, Egyptian Museum nearby

Four Seasons Hotel Cairo — First Residence

★★★★★ $400–$800 / night

One of Cairo's most prestigious addresses, the Four Seasons Cairo overlooks the Nile River and offers world-class luxury with fine dining restaurants, a full-service spa, an outdoor pool, and stunning Nile views.

Features: Nile views, fine dining, spa & wellness, business center, concierge service

Luxor Hotels

Sofitel Winter Palace — Luxor

★★★★★ $200–$450 / night

One of Egypt's most historic hotels, opened in 1886. Agatha Christie wrote "Death on the Nile" in this hotel. It features Victorian architecture, lush gardens, and is located steps from the Luxor Temple.

Features: Historic Victorian palace, Nile views, beautiful gardens, 2 restaurants, outdoor pool

Hurghada & Red Sea Hotels

Steigenberger Al Dau Beach Hotel — Hurghada

★★★★★ $120–$280 / night

One of Hurghada's finest beach resorts with direct Red Sea access. Perfect for families and divers. The resort offers a private sandy beach, multiple swimming pools, water sports, and a diving center.

Features: Private beach, diving center, 6 pools, all-inclusive option, kids' club

Booking Tips

Get the most out of your hotel stay in Egypt

1 Book October–April for the best weather — peak tourist season.
2 Book early — Nile cruise cabins and popular hotels sell out months in advance.
3 Smaller hotels and guesthouses may offer negotiable rates.
4 Stay near the Nile in Cairo for easy access to many attractions.
5 Red Sea resorts typically offer excellent all-inclusive deals.
6 Pay in Egyptian Pounds locally for better exchange rates.
